## A/B TESTING FRAMEWORK

### What to Test (from CRUCIBLE)

**Thumbnails (Highest Impact):**
- Face vs. no face (test every video)
- Text vs. no text
- Color variations (yellow vs. red vs. white)
- Expression (shocked vs. happy vs. serious)

**Titles:**
- Question vs. statement
- Short vs. long
- Number vs. no number
- Tool name vs. benefit

**Content Length:**
- 8 min vs. 12 min (long-form)
- 30s vs. 60s (Shorts)

---

### Statistical Significance

**Minimum Samples:**
- Thumbnail: 1,000 views/version
- Title: 500 views/version
- Content length: 3 videos per format

**Decision Rule:**
If one version clearly wins after minimum samples, go with it.
Don't over-test. Move on to next variable.

---

### Weekly A/B Testing Schedule

**Week 1:** Thumbnail face test (with vs. without)
**Week 2:** Title question test (question vs. statement)
**Week 3:** Color test (yellow vs. red accent)
**Week 4:** Length test (8 min vs. 12 min)

**Track results in:**
`05_ANALYTICS/A-B Test Results/`

## COPYRIGHT & COMPLIANCE

### AI-Generated Content (from GOV)

**Legal Status (2025):**
- AI images/video: Public domain (no copyright)
- AI music: Check tool terms
- AI scripts: Can't copyright AI content itself

**Fair Use for Educational:**
- Showing AI tools: Legal (educational)
- Commentary on AI: Legal (transformative)
- Reacting to AI content: Legal (if you add value)
- Reposting AI art: Risky (without transformation)

**Best Practice:**
- Always add value (commentary, education, transformation)
- Never repost AI content without adding your perspective
- Disclose AI-generated content (transparent)

---

**Safe Options:**
- YouTube Audio Library (free, safe)
- TikTok Commercial Sounds (free)
- Instagram Music (licensed)
- Epidemic Sound ($15/month)
